Frequently Asked Questions

What are the benefits of studying in the UK over India?

Better facilities, more esteemed teaching faculties, and degrees that count for more in Western society are just three benefits of studying in the UK.

What is the equivalent GCSE level in India?

India has two national boards (CBSE and ISC) and several regional boards, all of which conduct GCSE equivalent examinations at the end of Year 10.

Is an Indian degree valid in the UK?

Following a historic agreement between the UK and Indian governments, both countries will now recognise undergraduate and postgraduate degrees. This means that an Indian student with a degree from one of the Indian colleges will now be eligible to pursue higher studies in the UK and vice versa.

Is UK education the best in the world?

The UK has regularly been named the best country in the world for education and has long been considered one of the best nations for obtaining a world-class education and degree.

What is the UK higher education system like?

Compared to other global countries with regional differences and can be confusing to overseas students, higher education in the UK is simple to understand in terms of structure and grading. At every stage, international students are welcomed and made to feel at home.

How is the UK for Indian students?

Indian students are made to feel at home in the UK from the moment they step foot here. The UK is home to many Indian communities, and various clubs and societies at all UK institutions will ensure you make friends quickly.

Why are Indian students choosing to study in the UK?

Great career prospects, fantastic teaching and the post-study work visa are just three reasons why so many Indian students choose to study in the UK.

How do we compare UK universities and their courses?

Students can compare universities and courses in the UK by checking out the latest UK University Rankings and finding courses via our in-depth course search.

The Differences between UK and Indian Education

Education in India

SSC

The Secondary School Certificate is a public examination taken by students after completing at least ten years of schooling in the following subjects: languages (English & 1 Regional Language), Social Studies (SST), Mathematics, and Science (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology).

A new 9-point grading system has been introduced for this level: A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2, D, E1, E2.

HSC

The Higher Secondary Certificate is a two-year study in science, commerce, and arts at a school. Results are awarded in percentages as below:

  • Distinction – 75%+
  • First Class – 60%
  • Second Class – 50%
  • Pass – 33%

Bachelor’s Degree in India

This involves 3/4 years of study in a university with specialisation in an area like Commerce and Economics, Management, Computing, Arts, Engineering, or Science.

Degrees are awarded as follows:

  • Distinction
  • First Class
  • Second Class
  • Pass

Education in the UK

GCSE

General Certificate of Secondary Education: Level 2 of the NQF Framework for GCSEs grades A* – C. Students can take up to 9-12 GCSEs, including English and Maths, at A*- C.

GCSEs are available in over 40 academic and nine ‘applied’ subjects. The applied subjects are focused on wider work areas, such as engineering. When you take GCSEs, you can achieve pass grades from A* to G. If you don’t achieve the minimum pass mark to get a grade G, then a U grade is awarded, which means ‘unclassified’.

Higher tier (difficult level) exams lead to grades A* – D

Foundation tier exams lead to grades C – G

A-Levels

A-levels comprise the AS (advanced subsidiary) level and the A2. Each year’s grade makes up 50 per cent of the overall A-level grade. This forms level 3 of the NQF qualifications framework.

Year 1: AS-Level—Students can study the AS-Level as either a free-standing qualification or the first half of the full A-level.

Year 2: A2—In year two of study, you take the A2 – this is not a separate qualification, but the second half of the A-Level. The A2 is designed to deepen the knowledge you gained during the AS Level.

Colleges of further education and sixth-form colleges also offer academic, technical and vocational courses for people over 16.

Students can also choose to take the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma. This programme is an internationally recognised qualification for students aged 16 to 19. It is level 3 of the NQF qualifications framework and is focused on studying various subjects, including languages, science, maths, history, arts and geography.

Undergraduate UK Study

Universities and Colleges Admission Service (UCAS)

UCAS manages applications to higher education courses in the UK and processes more than two million applications for full-time undergraduate courses yearly. StudyIn uses UCAS for undergraduate applications on behalf of Indian students.

Foundation Study in the UK

Students can opt to study a Foundation Degree (Level 5 of the FHEQ / NQF qualifications framework), a university-level qualification involving the first two years of an honours degree. Upon obtaining a Foundation Degree, students may ‘top-up’ their qualification to an honours degree.

Undergraduate studies in the UK take three years to complete in most cases, leading to a qualification such as a Bachelor of Arts (BA) or a Bachelor of Science (BSc) – level 6 of NQF/ FHEQ. You will need at least four years of study if you choose a sandwich programme involving practical work accompanied by coursework.

Grades and their equivalent percentages:

Grade Equivalent Percentage
First 70%+
Upper-second (2:1) 60–69%
Lower-second (2:2) 50–59%
Third 40–49%
Pass 35–40%
Fail Below 35%

A third or above means you receive a bachelor’s degree with honours.

Undergraduate Study in India

After completing 12 years of education, students can apply for undergraduate degrees at universities or colleges. Undergraduate study in India typically consists of a 3-year Bachelor’s degree and a 4-year programme for a professional degree, such as a Bachelor of Engineering (B.E) or a Bachelor of Medicine or a Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS).

Admission is based on the student’s performance in their final secondary school examinations, such as the Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) or the Indian School Certificate (ISC). Some courses may have additional entrance exams or require minimum grades in specific subjects.

Postgraduate UK Study

Master’s Study

After completing an undergraduate degree, students can study for a postgraduate diploma, postgraduate certificate or the more traditional Master’s degree. Different types of postgraduate degrees include:

  • MA: Master of Arts
  • MSc: Master of Science
  • MBA: Master of Business Administration
  • LLM: Master of Laws
  • MEd: Master of Education
  • MPhil: Master of Philosophy
  • MRes: Master of Research

Grades are normally awarded as follows:

  • Distinction
  • Merit
  • Pass
  • Fail

Doctorates/PhD

A doctorate qualification or Research degree (Level 8 of the NQF/ FHEQ qualifications framework) allows you to undertake an original piece of research. Completing a PhD usually takes at least three years of full-time study. Doctorates are normally awarded as either a pass or fail, sometimes with distinction.

Postgraduate Study in India

Master’s Study

Postgraduate study in India typically consists of a two-year Master’s degree and a three-year professional degree, such as a Master of Business Administration (MBA) or a Master of Technology (M.Tech).

To be eligible for postgraduate study, students must have completed an undergraduate degree from a recognised university or college in India. Admissions to postgraduate courses is based on the student’s performance in their undergraduate degree and their scores on entrance exams like the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) for technical courses or the Common Admission Test (CAT) for management programmes.

Doctorates/PhD

A PhD in India typically takes 3-5 years to complete, depending on the field of study and the candidate’s research progress. To be eligible for a PhD in India, a student must have completed a Master’s degree from a recognised university or college. PhD students are typically required to submit a thesis or dissertation representing their original research and making a significant contribution to their field of study.

Education in Scotland

The Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA) provides syllabuses for years S3-S6 in secondary school. “Standard Grade” courses are taken by students, including studying years S3-S4. These are equivalent to the Indian SSC – 10th STD and the UK’s GCSE levels.

Grades range from 1 (highest) to 7 (lowest). Grade 3 and above is considered a pass.

Years S5 and S6 are 1-year courses, harder than GCSEs but easier than A-Levels. Therefore, some feel these courses are insufficient training for university, so Scottish university degrees generally last for one more year than English universities.

Grades range from A (highest) to C, which is a pass, and D, which is a near pass.

The Advanced Higher is taken by the ablest S6 students, where students focus on one or two subjects. These papers are graded A-E with credit values similar to A-Level in the UK.
