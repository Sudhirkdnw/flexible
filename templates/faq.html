The Differences between UK and Indian Education

Education in India

SSC

The Secondary School Certificate is a public examination taken by students after completing at least ten years of schooling in the following subjects: languages (English & 1 Regional Language), Social Studies (SST), Mathematics, and Science (Physics, Chemistry, and Biology).

A new 9-point grading system has been introduced for this level: A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2, D, E1, E2.

HSC

The Higher Secondary Certificate is a two-year study in science, commerce, and arts at a school. Results are awarded in percentages as below:

  • Distinction – 75%+
  • First Class – 60%
  • Second Class – 50%
  • Pass – 33%

Bachelor’s Degree in India

This involves 3/4 years of study in a university with specialisation in an area like Commerce and Economics, Management, Computing, Arts, Engineering, or Science.

Degrees are awarded as follows:

  • Distinction
  • First Class
  • Second Class
  • Pass

Education in the UK (Offered Online)

GCSE

General Certificate of Secondary Education: Level 2 of the NQF Framework for GCSEs grades A* – C. Students can take up to 9-12 GCSEs, including English and Maths, at A*- C, all delivered through our online platform with live support.

GCSEs are available in over 40 academic and nine ‘applied’ subjects. The applied subjects are focused on wider work areas, such as engineering. When you take GCSEs, you can achieve pass grades from A* to G. If you don’t achieve the minimum pass mark to get a grade G, then a U grade is awarded, which means ‘unclassified’.

Higher tier (difficult level) exams lead to grades A* – D

Foundation tier exams lead to grades C – G

A-Levels

A-levels comprise the AS (advanced subsidiary) level and the A2, delivered online with interactive lessons and live doubt sessions. Each year’s grade makes up 50 per cent of the overall A-level grade. This forms level 3 of the NQF qualifications framework.

Year 1: AS-Level—Students can study the AS-Level as either a free-standing qualification or the first half of the full A-level, with live tutor support.

Year 2: A2—In year two, students take the A2, deepening knowledge gained during the AS Level, supported by online resources and live sessions.

Colleges of further education and sixth-form colleges also offer academic, technical, and vocational courses for people over 16, which we provide online.

Students can also choose to take the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma online, an internationally recognised qualification for students aged 16 to 19, covering subjects like languages, science, maths, history, arts, and geography.

Undergraduate UK Study (Online Pathways)

Universities and Colleges Admission Service (UCAS)

UCAS manages applications to higher education courses in the UK. Our online school supports Indian students with UCAS applications for undergraduate studies, ensuring a smooth transition to UK universities.

Foundation Study in the UK

Students can opt for an online Foundation Degree (Level 5 of the FHEQ / NQF), a university-level qualification covering the first two years of an honours degree, with the option to ‘top-up’ to an honours degree.

Undergraduate studies in the UK take three years to complete, leading to qualifications like a Bachelor of Arts (BA) or Bachelor of Science (BSc) – level 6 of NQF/ FHEQ. Sandwich programmes with practical work take four years.

Grades and their equivalent percentages:

Grade Equivalent Percentage
First 70%+
Upper-second (2:1) 60–69%
Lower-second (2:2) 50–59%
Third 40–49%
Pass 35–40%
Fail Below 35%

A third or above means you receive a bachelor’s degree with honours.

Undergraduate Study in India

After completing 12 years of education, students can apply for undergraduate degrees at universities or colleges. Undergraduate study in India typically consists of a 3-year Bachelor’s degree and a 4-year programme for a professional degree, such as a Bachelor of Engineering (B.E) or a Bachelor of Medicine or a Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS).

Admission is based on the student’s performance in their final secondary school examinations, such as the Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) or the Indian School Certificate (ISC). Some courses may have additional entrance exams or require minimum grades in specific subjects.

Postgraduate UK Study (Online Pathways)

Master’s Study

After completing an undergraduate degree, students can pursue online postgraduate diplomas, certificates, or Master’s degrees, such as MA, MSc, MBA, LLM, MEd, MPhil, or MRes, with live doubt-clearing support.

Grades are normally awarded as follows:

  • Distinction
  • Merit
  • Pass
  • Fail

Doctorates/PhD

An online doctorate (Level 8 of the NQF/ FHEQ) involves original research over at least three years, with pass/fail or distinction grades, supported by virtual mentorship.

Postgraduate Study in India

Master’s Study

Postgraduate study in India typically consists of a two-year Master’s degree and a three-year professional degree, such as a Master of Business Administration (MBA) or a Master of Technology (M.Tech).

To be eligible for postgraduate study, students must have completed an undergraduate degree from a recognised university or college in India. Admissions to postgraduate courses is based on the student’s performance in their undergraduate degree and their scores on entrance exams like the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) for technical courses or the Common Admission Test (CAT) for management programmes.

Doctorates/PhD

A PhD in India typically takes 3-5 years to complete, depending on the field of study and the candidate’s research progress. To be eligible for a PhD in India, a student must have completed a Master’s degree from a recognised university or college. PhD students are typically required to submit a thesis or dissertation representing their original research and making a significant contribution to their field of study.

Education in Scotland (Online Option)

Our online platform offers the Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA) curriculum for years S3-S6, equivalent to Indian SSC and UK GCSEs, with grades from 1 (highest) to 7 (lowest), where 3 and above is a pass.

Years S5 and S6 courses, harder than GCSEs but easier than A-Levels, are delivered online, preparing students for Scottish university degrees, which last one year longer than English degrees.

The Advanced Higher, for the ablest S6 students, focuses on one or two subjects, graded A-E, and is offered online with live support.
